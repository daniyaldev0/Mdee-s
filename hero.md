# Hero Redesign

Read PROJECT_CONTEXT.md.

Continue from the existing project.

Modify ONLY the hero section.

## Goal

Redesign the hero to create a stronger first impression and increase conversions.

The current hero feels empty and does not immediately encourage visitors to order.

Do not redesign the rest of the website.

---

## Hero Content

Replace the current layout with a premium restaurant hero.

Include

• Strong headline
• Supporting paragraph
• Two CTA buttons
• Premium food composition
• Trust indicators

---

## Headline

Use a bold, conversion-focused headline.

Example direction

"Fresh. Fast. Unforgettable."

or

"Lahore's Favorite Late-Night Pizza."

The headline should immediately communicate quality and encourage ordering.

---

## Supporting Text

Add a short paragraph explaining MDEE'S.

Mention

- Fresh ingredients
- Handmade pizzas
- Burgers
- Shawarma
- Fast service
- Late-night availability

Keep it concise.

---

## CTA Buttons

Primary

Order on WhatsApp

Secondary

Browse Menu

The primary CTA should receive the most visual emphasis.

---

## Trust Section

Below the buttons, add small trust indicators.

Examples

Fresh Ingredients

Open 3:30 PM – 3:30 AM

Foodpanda Available

Fast Delivery

Use simple icons.

---

## Hero Visual

Create a premium composition using multiple featured products instead of a single pizza.

Examples

- Signature Pizza
- Zinger Burger
- Shawarma
- Flaming Wings
- Pasta

Arrange them in a layered composition with subtle floating effects.

The logo should support the hero, not dominate it.

---

## Layout

Desktop

Text on the left.

Food visuals on the right.

Mobile

Headline first.

Buttons second.

Food image below.

---

## Animations

Use subtle fade and slide animations.

Keep interactions smooth.

Avoid excessive effects.

---

## Requirements

- Preserve branding.
- Reuse existing styles where possible.
- Do not modify other sections.
- Keep performance optimized.

The hero should immediately make visitors want to explore the menu or place an order.
